Bedside Beacon

As part of a 4-person team, designed and built a laser-based point-to-point communication device for hospital patients with limited speech or mobility. Solely responsible for all firmware, implementing a T9-style multi-tap encoding scheme on an ESP32 that converts keypad input to ASCII and transmits it over a 650nm laser to a photodiode receiver. Optimized bit transmission timing to cut LCD display lag from 3,576ms to 615ms, and the system achieved reliable transmission across 4–8 meters with an 83% success rate at up to 15 feet. Both units run fully standalone on 9V batteries with no computer connection required.

Skills: C++, Arduino, ESP32, Embedded Systems, Signal Encoding, Circuit Design, Hardware Prototyping
